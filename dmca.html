IP Infringement & DMCA Policy

Effective 20 July 2026

How to report content on Kinly that infringes your intellectual property rights, and what happens after you report it.

Note: Kinly's circles are small and private by design — content isn't publicly visible outside a user's own Growth Circle. This policy exists so there's a clear process on record regardless, matching what app stores and copyright law generally expect from any app that lets people upload content.

Our policy

We respect the intellectual property rights of others and expect users of Kinly to do the same. We'll respond to clear, well-founded notices of alleged copyright or trademark infringement involving content uploaded to Kinly — most relevantly, images added to a circle's Vision Board.

How to submit a claim

If you believe content on Kinly infringes your copyright or other intellectual property rights, send a notice to the email below including:

  • A description of the copyrighted work or other IP you believe has been infringed.
  • Where the infringing content appears in Kinly (e.g. which circle, which feature — as specifically as you're able to identify it).
  • Your contact information (name, email address).
  • A statement that you have a good-faith belief the use is not authorized by the copyright/IP owner, their agent, or the law.
  • A statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright/IP owner or authorized to act on their behalf.
  • Your physical or electronic signature.

Notices that don't include enough information to identify and locate the content, or to verify your claim, may not be actionable.

What happens next

Upon receiving a valid notice, we will review it and, where warranted, remove or disable access to the reported content. Because Vision Board images and other user content generally live inside a single private circle, removal is typically limited to that specific content rather than a platform-wide takedown.

Counter-notification

If your content was removed and you believe this was a mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notice to the email below including your contact information, an identification of the removed content, and a statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good-faith belief the content was removed in error. We'll evaluate counter-notices on a case-by-case basis.

Repeat infringers

We may suspend or terminate the accounts of users found to repeatedly infringe others' intellectual property rights.
